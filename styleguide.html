We maintain the farm as an ecological unit. Managing it as an entire ecosystem that the livestock are part of and exist within. The ecosystem is designed so that the livestock are allowed to exhibit their natural behavioural needs, one corresponding to the environment which they are adapted to through evolution.

Livestock should be allowed to perform species-specific behaviour and be fed according to their physiological needs
— M. Vaarst 2005.

When in a complete functioning wild ecosystem, large herbivores graze mixed grass swards. Their movements and grazing patterns are shaped by large predators.
